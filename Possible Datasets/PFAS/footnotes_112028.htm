PFAS in Community Water Systems | Community water systems (CWS) with detections of PFOS, PFAS, PFNA, PFBS, PFHxS, PFHpA

Data details

Footnotes

  1. Data for PFAS concentrations in drinking water for selected community water systems (CWS) were obtained from EPA's National Contaminant Occurrence Database (https://www.epa.gov/dwstandardsregulations/national-contaminant-occurrence-database-ncod#unreg) for the third Unregulated Contaminant Monitoring Rule (UCMR 3). A summary of the UCMR 3 data and analytical results is available here: https://www.epa.gov/sites/production/files/2017-02/documents/ucmr3-data-summary-january-2017.pdf.
  2. Not all CWS were required to sample for PFAS chemicals. Those CWS that were required to sample, sampled twice if the water source was groundwater and four times, if the water source was surface water.
  3. Sampling for UCMR 3 at selected CWS occurred from January 2013 to December 2015 and PFAS results are available only for this timeframe. Current levels of PFAS are not available from the National Contaminant Occurrence Database.

  4. In most cases, CWS locations correspond to the centroid of the service area for each CWS. About 50% of the CWS locations were derived from the ZIP code(s) served as reported in the UCMR 3 data. The remaining CWS locations were derived from information provided by state and local Tracking Programs or the centroid of the principal county served as reported in EPA's Safe Drinking Water Information System. Data linkages and spatial analysis for deriving the CWS locations were provided by ATSDR's Science Support Branch.

Why was the data set created?

Purpose

These measures provide information to the public on the water systems where PFAS chemicals were detected, and where the two chemicals with the EPA health advisory (PFOA and PFOS) were found in exceedance of that level. The measures also provide information on the water systems where samples were taken and no chemicals were detected.

How was the data set created?

Derivation of measure

Measures were created from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's (EPA) Third Unregulated Contaminant Monitoring Rule (UCMR3) sampling data for per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS).

  1. Community water systems (CWS) tested where the sum of PFOA and PFAS was at or above the 70 ppt EPA lifetime health advisory (LTHA).
  2. CWS tested that had detections of any of the 6 PFAS chemicals
  3. All CWS that sampled for PFAS chemicals (not all CWS were tested for PFAS) including those with samples that had non-detectable concentrations.

What are the limitations of the data set?

Unregulated drinking water contaminants are sampled two or four times in a 12 consecutive month period. PFAS chemicals were sampled between 2013 and 2015.

How should these data be used?

Use Constraints

Concentrations in drinking water cannot be directly converted to human exposure, because water consumption varies by climate, level of physical activity, and between people.
